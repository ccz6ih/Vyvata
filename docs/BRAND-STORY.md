## Why Vyvata

In Ayurveda, *vata* is the vital force — breath, circulation, the current that moves through the body and carries everything else with it. It's one of the oldest ideas in human medicine: that clarity of movement is the foundation of health.

We didn't take the name from a dictionary. We built it — evoking that older idea, because modern supplement wellness has drifted so far from it that the word for what we do didn't really exist yet. An independent voice. A clear current through a murky industry. A standard you can actually trust.

Vyvata is the integrity score for what you put in your body. Evidence-graded. Compliance-checked. Accountable to you — not to the brands being scored.

---

## Honesty note (read before using this story)

The name **Vyvata** is a modern coinage. It *evokes* the Sanskrit concept of *vata* — it is not *derived* from it. There is no classical text in which the word "vyvata" appears. This distinction matters because:

1. If a scholar, practitioner, or reporter asks where "vyvata" appears in classical Sanskrit, the honest answer is: it doesn't. We built the word.
2. Overclaiming Sanskrit lineage — even implicitly — invites criticism from cultural authenticity auditors and Ayurvedic practitioners, and it's unnecessary. The brand is stronger for being honest.
3. The phrase "We didn't take the name from a dictionary. We built it" is load-bearing — it is the line that makes the story honest rather than cosplay.

**Do not edit this distinction out of any public-facing copy** without a deliberate conversation about brand integrity. Honest origin stories age well. Appropriated ones don't.

## Voice & tone guidelines

Vyvata writes like a reviewer, not a marketer. That means:

- **Declarative sentences.** "This product scored 85/100." Not: "This product has been carefully evaluated and received a rating of 85 out of 100."
- **Evidence-forward, never hype-forward.** No "revolutionary," "breakthrough," "cutting-edge," "game-changing." If the product is good, the score says so.
- **British English preference** where it doesn't fight American readers. "Analysed," "recognised," "centre" feels slightly more clinical and less Silicon Valley. (This is a preference, not a rule — don't let it make copy feel affected.)
- **Present tense.** "Vyvata scores products." Not: "Vyvata will score products."
- **No emoji in product-facing copy.** Reserved for internal docs or informal channels.
- **Concrete over abstract.** "Active FDA warning letter from March 2024" beats "has had regulatory issues."
- **Respect the reader.** Practitioners and informed consumers are the target audience. Don't explain what an RCT is in body copy. Link to a glossary if needed.

### Words we use
Integrity. Evidence. Verification. Independence. Transparency. Accountability. Standard. Audit.

### Words we avoid
Clean, pure, natural, organic (when unqualified), premium, elite (except as tier name), revolutionary, proprietary (except in criticism of proprietary blends), synergy (unless clinically justified), optimize, biohack, stack (verb form — reserved for noun use by practitioners).

## The question behind the name

If someone asks "what does Vyvata stand for?" — meaning, are the letters an acronym? — the answer is no, and that's deliberate. Acronyms date a brand (IBM, NASA, BMW are from an era when spelling out what you did was part of the pitch). Modern brands in health and wellness work differently: the name is a *feeling*, the meaning is something you earn over time.

If someone wants a principled answer rather than an etymological one, here are the six principles that govern how Vyvata operates — they aren't an acronym but they're the honest answer to "what does Vyvata stand for":

1. **Verify what's claimed.** Every claim checked against evidence.
2. **Yield nothing to opacity.** Missing data scores zero, not neutral. (Except sustainability — see the scoring rubric.)
3. **Validate against evidence.** PubMed, FDA, NSF, USP — not marketing copy.
4. **Audit continuously.** Scores are living documents. When evidence changes, scores change.
5. **Trust, earned — not given.** No pay-to-play. No sponsored rankings.
6. **Accountability to the consumer, always.** Vyvata's customer is the person reading the score, not the brand being scored.

These six can be referenced as "the six principles" or "the Vyvata Standards" in copy where it makes sense. They should not be forced into a V-Y-V-A-T-A acronym on public-facing surfaces.
